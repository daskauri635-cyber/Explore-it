Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi, born in 1869 in Porbandar, India, was a visionary leader who pioneered the philosophy of nonviolent resistance, known as satyagraha. Gandhi's unwavering commitment to truth, simplicity, and justice inspired global movements for civil rights and freedom, influencing leaders like Martin Luther King Jr. and Nelson Mandela. Revered as the "Father of the Nation" in India, his legacy continues to resonate as a symbol of moral courage and peaceful activism.
