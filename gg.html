Prithvi Narayan Shah

Prithvi Narayan Shah was the founding monarch of modern Nepal and a visionary leader who unified the fragmented kingdoms of the region into a single nation. Born in 1723 in Gorkha, he ascended the throne at a young age and embarked on a strategic campaign to consolidate power, culminating in the conquest of the Kathmandu Valley in 1768. His leadership laid the foundation for a sovereign Nepal, free from colonial domination. Prithvi Narayan Shah emphasized national unity, self-reliance, and cultural preservation, famously describing Nepal as a "yam between two boulders"—a metaphor for its geopolitical position between China and India. His legacy remains deeply revered in Nepalese history.
