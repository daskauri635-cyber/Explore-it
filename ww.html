Neil Armstrong

Neil Armstrong was an American astronaut, aerospace engineer, and naval aviator best known for being the first human to walk on the Moon. Born in 1930 in Wapakoneta, Ohio, Armstrong developed a passion for flight at an early age and became a test pilot before joining NASA. On July 20, 1969, during the Apollo 11 mission, he descended from the lunar module and famously declared, “That’s one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind,” as he stepped onto the Moon’s surface. His historic achievement marked a defining moment in space exploration and human history. Armstrong remained a humble figure throughout his life, dedicated to science, education, and inspiring future generations.
