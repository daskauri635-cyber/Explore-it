Sally Ride

Sally Ride was a trailblazing American astronaut and physicist who made history as the first American woman to travel into space. Born in 1951 in Los Angeles, California, she joined NASA in 1978 and flew aboard the space shuttle *Challenger* in 1983. At just 32 years old, she became the youngest American astronaut to go to space at the time. Beyond her spaceflight achievements, Ride was a passionate advocate for science education, especially for girls. She co-founded Sally Ride Science to promote STEM learning and authored several books aimed at inspiring young minds. Her legacy continues to shine as a symbol of courage, intellect, and the power of breaking barriers.
