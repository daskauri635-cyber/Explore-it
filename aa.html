Greta Thunberg

Greta Thunberg is a Swedish environmental activist who became a global voice for climate action at an astonishingly young age. Born in 2003, she first gained international attention in 2018 when she began skipping school to protest outside the Swedish parliament, demanding stronger climate policies. Her solo protest sparked a worldwide movement known as “Fridays for Future,” inspiring millions of young people to advocate for environmental justice. Greta is known for her powerful speeches at major forums like the United Nations, where she challenges world leaders to act urgently on climate change. Her unwavering commitment and moral clarity have made her a symbol of youth-led activism and ecological responsibility.
