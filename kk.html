Wangari Maathai

Wangari Maathai was a pioneering Kenyan environmentalist, political activist, and Nobel Peace Prize laureate who championed sustainability, democracy, and women's rights. Born in 1940 in Nyeri, Kenya, she became the first African woman to earn a Ph.D. and later founded the Green Belt Movement in 1977, which mobilized communities—especially women—to plant millions of trees across Kenya to combat deforestation and restore ecosystems. Her activism extended beyond environmental issues, advocating for human rights and democratic governance. In 2004, she became the first African woman to receive the Nobel Peace Prize. Maathai’s legacy lives on as a powerful symbol of ecological stewardship and social justice.
